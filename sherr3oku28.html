Introduction

In recent years, there has been an increasingly rapid advance in the field of machine-learning which led to a large influence on business planning and business strategy for almost all industries.  Machine-learning has been used to help companies and organization conduct better decision makings in a data-scientific manner.  There are numerous challenges to decision makings due to an aggressive and uncertainty of market behavior in the business environment \cite{zhao_sales_2017}.  For example,  a fashion industry that has production plants in Asian countries may need to decide the production plans long before the accurate information of future demand is available. Sales forecasting is one of the technologies that utilize machine-learning to solve these challenges. A Sales forecast predicts the future value of sales over a period of time.  It is a highly complicated problem due to the influence of internal and external environments. Thus, the forecast accuracy becomes a key aspect of sales forecasting. Inaccurate forecasts may lead to stock-outs or overstock inventories which affects the efficiency of the business decision and even result in losses for the companies  \cite{Beheshti_Kashi_2014}.  Therefore,  sales forecasting is an increasingly important part of the success and performance of companies. 
Sale forecasting mainly depends on past historical sales data. Past historical sales data are classified as a time series data. Tak-chung Fu (2010) defined time series as sets of data points which takes into account of temporal ordering \cite{Fu_2011}.  Because of the time series characteristic,  sales forecasting widely use traditional time series analysis methods which are based on mathematical and statistical models. Exponential smoothing, autoregressive integrated moving average (ARIMA) and regression models are examples of statistical approach in sales forecasting.  In statistical time series analysis approach, it assumes that the time series data is linear.
